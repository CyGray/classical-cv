# IW-FCV 2026
## The 32nd International Workshop on Frontiers of Computer Vision
**September 30th - October 2nd, 2026**  
**Tokushima University, Tokushima, Japan**

---

### 📢 Call for Papers
* **Workshop Website:** [https://sites.me.tokushima-u.ac.jp/IW-FCV2026/](https://sites.me.tokushima-u.ac.jp/IW-FCV2026/)
* **Format:** Hybrid (Face-to-face and online participation)

The International Workshop on Frontiers of Computer Vision (IW-FCV) is a historic workshop that provides an exciting venue for inter-communication and discussion through many presentations of advanced research from worldwide research communities in computer vision theory, applications, deep learning, and big data.

*Note: The IW-FCV has traditionally been held in February, but its schedule has been changed starting from 2026.*

---

### 🎯 Related Topics
Topics of interest include fundamentals and practical applications of image processing, computer vision, and deep learning. Specific topics include, but are not limited to:

#### 1. Fundamentals and Theory
* Image filtering, Image enhancement, Image restoration, Image transformation
* Stochastic vision, Stereo vision, Scale space analysis, Hough transform
* Morphological processing, Image sequence analysis, Image processing architecture
* Representation, Color and illumination analysis, Vision geometry, Image coding, Error analysis

#### 2. Computer Vision and Image Analysis
* Shape-from-X, Active vision, Image/Scene segmentation
* Object detection and tracking, Visual language and description
* Deep learning for computer vision, Machine & semantic learning for computer vision
* Integration of deep learning and conventional methods
* Performance evaluation and datasets

#### 3. Applications
* Image/video search and retrieval, Video surveillance
* Computer vision application to AR/VR/MR/HR
* Smart factory, Healthcare, Intelligent transportation systems, Autonomous land vehicles
* Bio-medical image analysis, Agriculture, Smart home, Logistics and Distribution, Intelligent robots

#### 4. Recognition & Learning
* 2D/3D object recognition, Action and behavior recognition, Adversarial learning
* Face and gesture recognition, Human pose estimation
* Efficient training and inference methods, Explainable AI, Representation learning
* Scene analysis and understanding
* Transfer, low-shot, semi-, and unsupervised learning, Reinforcement learning

### 📄 Paper Submission & Post-Workshop Proceedings
The submission process has been updated for IW-FCV 2026. Authors are invited to submit papers in the following formats:

* **Long Paper (12–15 pages):** Eligible for Oral / Poster / Oral & Poster presentations.
* **Short Paper (2–11 pages):** Eligible for Poster presentation.

#### Presentation at the Workshop
All submissions will undergo a pre-workshop screening. Accepted short papers will be presented as posters, while accepted long papers will be presented in oral sessions, poster sessions, or both.

#### Post-Workshop Proceedings
Authors of long papers are required to indicate at submission whether they wish their papers to be considered for the post-workshop proceedings. Only papers opting for proceedings consideration will undergo a post-workshop review, and selected high-quality papers will be accepted for publication in the proceedings. Please refer to the [official website](https://sites.me.tokushima-u.ac.jp/IW-FCV2026/) for further details.
